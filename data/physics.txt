[FACT: PRINCIPLE]
In classical mechanics, all objects fall at the same rate in a vacuum regardless of their mass.
This principle was demonstrated by Galileo Galilei and is explained by Newtonâ€™s laws of motion.

The gravitational force acting on an object is proportional to its mass, described by the equation F = mg,
where m is mass and g is the acceleration due to gravity. When calculating acceleration (a = F / m),
the mass cancels out, resulting in the same acceleration for all objects.

[FACT: EXPERIMENT]
This means that in the absence of air resistance, a feather and a hammer will fall at the same rate.
This phenomenon was famously demonstrated on the Moon during the Apollo 15 mission.
